E-Commerce


Electronic Commerce

E-commerce (Electronic Commerce) refers to the buying and selling of goods, services, or information over the internet and other electronic networks. It involves the exchange of products or services between businesses, consumers, or both, using digital platforms and technologies. In simpler terms, it's any commercial transaction that happens online. Instead of going to a physical store, you use a computer, tablet, or smartphone to browse products, place orders, and make payments. Common types of e-commerce are: B2B e-commerce, B2C e-commerce and C2C e-commerce.


Online shopping

Online shopping represents the most prevalent form of e-commerce, directly connecting companies with their end consumers. This popular method allows individuals to purchase products through a company's dedicated online shop, which can often be accessed via websites or convenient mobile applications. These digital storefronts typically display a comprehensive range of products offered by the company, complete with detailed pricing information. Once a selection is made and an order is placed, the purchased items are then prepared and dispatched, usually arriving at the customer's doorstep in a package delivered by a courier or the postal service.

The pros and the cons for the consumer...

Online shopping offers distinct advantages for customers, notably lower prices and the convenience of 24/7 availability, allowing purchases from virtually anywhere. However, a significant drawback is the inability to physically examine, touch, or try on products before making a purchase.

...and for companies

The pros for the companies are:
- Setting up an online shop is usually very cheap or even free
- The online shop mostly works automatically
- With an online shop, you can sell to people located anywhere

The cons for the companies are:
- The products in online shops have to be stored somewhere
- Online shops have to compete with shops all around the world
- In online shops customers can't talk to someone before buying a product


Setting up an online shop

Setting up an online shop is streamlined by using an e-commerce platform. These platforms enable businesses to quickly launch a website where customers can easily find and purchase products. The primary task for the company then becomes shipping the product directly from their warehouse to the customer. Popular examples of such platforms include Shopify, which is known for being very affordable, and Amazon, where the associated fees are considerably higher.

B2B e-commerce

B2B e-commerce (Business-to-Business e-commerce) refers to the online exchange of products, services, or information between two businesses. In simpler terms, it's when one company sells something to another company using the internet. This is different from B2C (Business-to-Consumer) e-commerce, where a business sells directly to an individual customer. Key aspects of B2B e-commerce include: digital platform, business buyers, complex transactions, long-term relationships and automation.

Warehousing and storage

While often used interchangeably, "warehousing" and "storage" have distinct meanings, especially in the context of business and supply chain management. In its simplest form, storage is the act of keeping something in a safe place until it is needed. The primary focus of storage is simply to hold goods securely and protect them from damage, theft, or deterioration. Warehousing is a more comprehensive and organized process than simple storage. It involves the strategic management of goods within a dedicated facility (a warehouse) from the moment they arrive until they are ready to be shipped out. Warehousing is a critical part of the supply chain and goes beyond just holding items. All warehousing involves storage, but not all storage is warehousing. For example, a self-storage unit is for storage, but a large logistics center managing inventory for multiple clients is engaged in warehousing.
